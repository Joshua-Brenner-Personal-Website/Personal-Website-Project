Tamper Monkey Click Less
The Tampermonkey “Click Less” was a project inspired by an Amazon Software Developement II located in Vancouver that created a JavaScript script file that changed the behavior of one of the Trouble Ticket websites that my department commonly uses. Essentially, he made it more obvious/intuitive and reduced the workload of clicks for a repeated task.This inspired me to create similar functionality in several other sites that are commonly accessed, reducing clicks, reducing errors, and reducing risk of injury from repeated motions. Redundancy is for automation.

Options and their Normal Distribution
300 years, several generations of genius, and the early discovery of Brownian Motion culminated in a Nobel-Prize winning equation that explained, mathematically, the influences of factors contributing to the pricing of equity options: The Black-Scholes Equation.
Leveraging statistics and the probability of an “iron condor” multi-leg option strategy passing through a profitability point according to this normal distribution, I was able to isolate a trading strategy that allowed for consistent gains while limiting risk to a quantifiable acceptance.Now the fun part, integrating with a brokers API, I simplify the implementation down to a single click and let JavaScript do the work.

The Witch Hunt
A project I unapologetically call the Witch Hunt, this mess came about as a result of my concerns about a lack of accountability. While voicing these concerns to a senior ops manager and explaining the impact on customers as well as labor losses to track down and correct issues without having a method to provide feedback necessary to isolate the cause or correct behaviors, I found myself called out.
I was asked what I would propose as a solution and if I’d have the ability to implement my proposed solution… and here we are. Again I turn to Tampermonkey to leverage JavaScript to search for correlations in a database to track “touches” that turn into “troubles” in an effort to show inbound departmental issues that affect our ability to process outbound fulfillment.
